Case study 01 2023 · Lead Product Designer · Mentally Aware Nigeria Initiative

ITUNU APP 1.0

Crisis-support intake ran on Google Forms, with waits stretching from 3 hours to 5 days. I designed a counsellor case-management app, a WhatsApp onboarding bot and an admin dashboard — so people in crisis get help in minutes.

Read on Medium ↗

The problem

MANI — the largest provider of crisis services in Nigeria — relied on manual Google Forms intake, creating bottlenecks that delayed mental-health support by up to 5 days.

The solution

A three-part system: a case-management app for counsellors, an interactive WhatsApp bot for client onboarding, and a centralised admin dashboard.

My role

Lead Product Designer — research, ideation facilitation, flows, wireframes, design system, hi-fi prototypes and usability testing.

Timeline

~4 weeks — 1 week research, 1 day ideation, 1 week prototyping, 1 week testing & iteration.

01 Context & challenge

Mentally Aware Nigeria Initiative (MANI) offers mental-health counselling through WhatsApp, connecting skilled counsellors with those in need. Demand was escalating fast — but clients submitted their cases through Google Forms, and with a limited number of counsellors available, this manual process resulted in frustrating delays.

Wait times stretched from 3 hours to up to 5 days. For a person reaching out in crisis, that gap isn't an inconvenience — it can be the difference between getting help and giving up. The challenge: rebuild the intake and case-management experience so support arrives in minutes, while giving MANI's team the structure and data they'd never had.

02 User research

I dedicated a week to understanding every side of the service: 10 counsellor interviews and 50 client surveys, alongside sessions with MANI's in-house team. The research shaped detailed personas and three customer journey maps — one per stakeholder — so each group's pains and emotions stayed visible throughout the project.

03 Framing the problem

The research converged on three actionable How-Might-We questions:

HMW 01

How might we decrease client wait times and improve scheduling efficiency at MANI?

HMW 02

How might we implement an automated case-management system that enhances efficiency and client privacy?

HMW 03

How might we effectively gather actionable data from counselling sessions to drive improvements?

04 Ideation

I facilitated a 10-for-10 sketching workshop with the team — rapid divergence, then dot-voting to converge on the strongest concepts. The winning direction became the three-part solution: counsellor app, WhatsApp onboarding bot, and admin dashboard.

05 Flows & wireframes

Three user flows — client, counsellor and admin — mapped every step from first message to closed case, before a full set of low-fidelity wireframes locked the layouts.

06 Design system

A style guide and componentised UI kit — dashboard components, mobile components and a custom icon set — so the system outlives the file and MANI's future designers can build consistently.

07 Prototype & testing

High-fidelity prototypes for the mobile app and admin dashboard went into usability testing with real counsellors and clients. Findings fed a structured iteration pass — refining navigation, states and copy before handoff.
