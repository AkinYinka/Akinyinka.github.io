Case study 03 2020 · Product Designer · Guardian Nigeria

GUARDIAN REDESIGN

One of Nigeria's most established newspapers, redesigned end-to-end — from user research and article-page UX to a modern editorial design language readers actually stay for.

View on Behance ↗

The problem

A dated reading experience: cluttered layouts, weak hierarchy and an article page that made one of the country's most trusted mastheads feel harder to read online than in print.

The solution

A ground-up redesign of the news platform — modern editorial hierarchy, a calmer article-page UX, and a cohesive design language across sections.

My role

Product Designer at Guardian Nigeria — user research, wireframes, UI design and prototype. Tools: Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator.

Scope

Full platform — home, section fronts and the article page, designed and prototyped end-to-end in 2020.

01 The story

The Guardian has been part of Nigeria's national conversation since 1983. Its journalism carried authority — but its website didn't. Readers landed on dense pages where headlines, ads and widgets competed for attention, and the article page — where a news site lives or dies — buried the story it was meant to serve.

Working from user research and an audit of the existing experience, I rebuilt the reading experience around editorial hierarchy: clear typographic scale, generous whitespace, and an article page that puts the journalism first. Wireframes moved through iteration into a high-fidelity prototype covering the platform's core templates.

The full process — research, wireframes, and every screen — is in the case study below, exactly as published.
